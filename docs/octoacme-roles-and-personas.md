# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates Agile ceremonies, removes impediments, and coaches the team on continuous improvement practices. They act as a servant-leader to keep delivery flowing smoothly.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, backlog refinement, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove process blockers or external impediments
- Coach the team on Agile/Scrum practices and principles
- Shield the team from distractions during a sprint
- Track velocity and help the team improve its predictability

### Goals
- Enable a healthy, self-organizing delivery team
- Reduce lead time and cycle time through process improvement
- Foster a safe environment for feedback and experimentation

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retro)
- Impediment logs and action items shared with the team
- Regular syncs with Project Manager on cross-team blockers

### Interactions / Collaboration
- **With Developers:** Runs daily standups, coaches on Agile practices, removes blockers surfaced by the team.
- **With Product Managers:** Coordinates backlog refinement sessions and sprint planning; ensures stories are ready before a sprint starts.
- **With Project Managers:** Aligns on milestone risks, escalates impediments that require cross-team or stakeholder intervention, collaborates on risk register updates.

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centered interfaces and ensure the product meets usability standards. They bridge user research insights and engineering implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research, usability tests, and synthesize findings
- Define and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine requirements into designs
- Participate in acceptance reviews to verify design fidelity

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce re-work by catching usability issues before implementation
- Align design decisions with data-driven insights

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test reports shared with Data Analyst and Product Manager
- Async design handoffs via design files with annotations

### Interactions / Collaboration
- **With Developers:** Provides detailed design specs and assets; participates in sprint planning to clarify design requirements; reviews implementation during sprint review for design accuracy.
- **With Product Managers:** Partners on feature definition and acceptance criteria; validates designs against user needs and product goals during backlog refinement.
- **With Project Managers:** Flags design dependencies or risks (e.g., pending research) that may affect timelines; contributes to risk register entries related to UX scope.

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts measure project and product outcomes, build dashboards, and inform prioritization and decision-making with evidence.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key project and product metrics
- Build dashboards and reports for project health and feature impact
- Advise on hypothesis testing and A/B experiment design
- Identify delivery trends and surface risks to Project and Product Managers
- Support post-release analysis and contribute to retrospective insights

### Goals
- Provide timely, accurate data to support informed decisions
- Reduce reliance on intuition by making success criteria measurable
- Shorten feedback loops between delivery and outcome measurement

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews with Product Manager and Project Manager
- Dashboard links included in status updates and release notes
- Findings presented during retrospectives and sprint reviews

### Interactions / Collaboration
- **With Developers:** Collaborates on instrumentation and data collection requirements; reviews analytics implementations during sprint review.
- **With Product Managers:** Provides insights that inform backlog prioritization; co-owns success metrics and OKR tracking; supports release readiness reviews with outcome data.
- **With Project Managers:** Highlights delivery trends (e.g., velocity drops, defect rates) that signal risks; contributes data points to risk register and status reports.

---

## Stakeholder Champion

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Champion represents the interests of external and internal stakeholders, ensuring their needs are heard and alignment is maintained throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize feedback from stakeholder groups
- Facilitate stakeholder communications and briefings
- Escalate misalignment or emerging concerns early
- Track stakeholder commitments and follow-through on action items
- Ensure stakeholders are informed at key milestones (release, retrospective)

### Goals
- Maintain continuous stakeholder alignment to avoid late-stage surprises
- Build trust between the project team and stakeholder groups
- Accelerate decision-making by ensuring the right people are engaged at the right time

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder update presentations
- Escalation notices to Project Manager when alignment is at risk
- Meeting summaries and decision logs distributed after stakeholder sessions

### Interactions / Collaboration
- **With Developers:** Channels stakeholder feedback about user-facing behavior; ensures developer-facing impact from stakeholder decisions is communicated clearly.
- **With Product Managers:** Partners on roadmap communications; co-facilitates stakeholder alignment sessions; flags feedback that should influence backlog prioritization.
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinates stakeholder review schedules; supports risk escalation when stakeholder blockers threaten the plan; contributes to release readiness sign-off.
